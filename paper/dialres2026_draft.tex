\title{HeptaNER: A Neuro-Symbolic Pipeline and Benchmark\\for Classifying 16th-Century Heptanesian Notarial Acts}

\name{Stergios Kafouroutsos$^{1}$, Author Two$^{2}$}
\address{$^1$ Affiliation One \\
         $^2$ Affiliation Two \\
         \texttt{kafouroutsos@gmail.com}}

\abstract{
16th-century Greek notarial acts from Venetian-ruled Ionian Islands = underexplored resource for historical NLP.
We present HeptaNER: (a) a new digitised corpus of $\sim$950 acts from 5 notaries, (b) a 40-act benchmark annotated with a 3-tier legal genre taxonomy (17 core categories + extensions + hybrid tags), (c) a neuro-symbolic (NeSy) pipeline combining $\sim$70 deterministic rules encoding Venetian-Greek legal formulae with LLM classification.
Evaluation across 12 LLMs $\times$ 4 prompting strategies shows NeSy compresses the performance gap between frontier and small models from 47.5pp to 12.5pp.
An 8B-parameter model with NeSy (65.0\%) rivals frontier models without it; three models tie at 72.5\% with NeSy.
We release the corpus, benchmark, taxonomy, and symbolic rule engine as open resources for historical Greek NLP.
}

% ============================================================
\section{Introduction}
% ============================================================

\begin{itemize}
\item The Heptanese (Ionian Islands) under Venetian rule (13th--18th c.) produced a rich notarial tradition blending Greek, Latin, and Italian legal conventions
\item Notarial registers are a primary source for social, economic, and legal history of early modern Greece.thousands of acts survive, largely unclassified
\item Classifying these acts into legal genres (Venditio, Testamentum, Emphyteusis, etc.) is essential for historians but requires paleographic expertise and is slow to do manually
\item NLP approaches face challenges: non-standard orthography, code-switching (Greek/Latin/Italian), formulaic but variable legal language, domain vocabulary absent from LLM training data
\item We present HeptaNER, addressing this with three contributions:
  \begin{enumerate}
  \item A new digitised corpus of $\sim$950 notarial acts from 5 sixteenth-century Heptanesian notaries, plus a 40-act benchmark with 3-tier genre annotations
  \item A taxonomy of 17 core legal genres with extensions and hybrid tags, designed for the Venetian-Greek notarial tradition
  \item A neuro-symbolic pipeline that combines deterministic symbolic rules (encoding formulaic phrases, Venetian loanwords, legal terminology) with LLM-based classification
  \end{enumerate}
\item We evaluate 12 LLMs across 4 prompting strategies and show that symbolic knowledge injection dramatically reduces model-size dependency
\end{itemize}


% ============================================================
\section{Related Work}
% ============================================================

\begin{itemize}
\item Historical document classification: OCR + classification pipelines for medieval charters (Tilburg, Huygens Institute), genre detection in historical corpora
\item NLP for Greek: modern Greek NER and POS tagging exists, but historical/dialectal Greek is under-resourced; GreekBERT, work on Byzantine texts
\item Notarial act classification: mostly manual in historiography (Maltezou, Nikiforou, Karapidakis); no computational approach to Heptanesian notarial acts exists
\item Neuro-symbolic NLP: hybrid rule+neural systems for low-resource domains (DeepPavlov, Speer et al.); symbolic features as LLM prompt augmentation is under-explored
\item LLM evaluation on historical texts: recent work evaluating GPT-4 / Claude on archival material (Colavizza et al.), but not on Greek notarial acts
\item Dialectal NLP resources: existing work on Modern Greek dialects (Pontian, Cypriot, Cretan) but not on early modern Heptanesian administrative/legal Greek
\end{itemize}

\section{The HeptaNER Resource}
% ============================================================

% ------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{Corpus}
% ------------------------------------------------------------

\begin{itemize}
\item Source: 5 notarial registers from 16th-century Heptanesian notaries, transcribed from manuscript:
  \begin{itemize}
  \item ALEXAKIS: 101 acts
  \item FARMAKIS: 172 acts
  \item SPYRIS: 327 acts
  \item TOXOTIS: 182 acts
  \item VARAGAS: 125 acts
  \item Total: 907 acts (excluding Kartanos, a theological text)
  \end{itemize}
\item Language: early modern Greek with heavy Venetian/Latin legal vocabulary (e.g., ``οπληγάδο'' from It.\ \emph{obbligo}, ``κοντέντος'' from It.\ \emph{contento}, ``ρεβοκάρω'' from It.\ \emph{revocare})
\item Acts range from 50 to 500+ words; include dates (indiction system), witness lists, notarial formulas
\item The corpus is parsed and segmented but not genre-annotated.released as-is for the community
\item To our knowledge, the first computationally accessible corpus of Heptanesian notarial acts
\end{itemize}

% ------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{Taxonomy}
% ------------------------------------------------------------

\begin{itemize}
\item 17 core legal genre categories, organised in 6 thematic groups:
  \begin{enumerate}
  \item[I.] \emph{Contractus de rebus} (property): Venditio, Donatio, Permutatio, Emphyteusis, Locatio conductio rei
  \item[II.] \emph{Contractus de credito} (credit): Mutuum, Instrumentum obligationis, Quietantia, Pignus/Hypotheca
  \item[III.] \emph{Contractus operarum} (production): Locatio conductio operis, Societas, Praevenditio
  \item[IV.] \emph{Acta familiaris} (family/personal): Testamentum, Dos, Divisio/Partitio
  \item[V.] \emph{Acta procedurales} (procedural): Procuratio, Renuntiatio, Revocatio
  \item[VI.] \emph{Acta ecclesiastica}: Concessio ecclesiastica, Beneficium
  \end{enumerate}
\item Taxonomy designed to balance the Latin legal tradition with the specificities of Venetian-ruled Greek practice
\item Extension layer captures subtypes (e.g., emphyteusis perpetua, locatio vineae)
\item Hybrid layer captures cross-cutting features that span multiple core types
\item Presented in trilingual form: Latin / English / Greek
\end{itemize}


% ============================================================
\section{Methodology}
% ============================================================

% ------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{Symbolic Engine}
% ------------------------------------------------------------

\begin{itemize}
\item $\sim$70 hand-crafted genre-discriminative rules targeting formulaic phrases in notarial Greek
\item Rules are pattern-matching on Unicode-normalised text (NFD decomposition, lowercase, accent stripping)
\item Rule types:
  \begin{itemize}
  \item Venetian loanwords: ``οπληγάδο'' (\emph{obbligo}) $\rightarrow$ Mutuum/Instrumentum obligationis; ``κοντέντος'' (\emph{contento}) $\rightarrow$ Quietantia
  \item Latin legal formulae: ``εις τον αιώνα'' $\rightarrow$ Venditio (perpetuity clause); ``εις την ερχομένην εσωδίαν'' $\rightarrow$ Praevenditio (forward sale)
  \item Structural markers: ``αφήνω'' + ``ψυχή'' $\rightarrow$ Testamentum; ``εξουσιωτική'' $\rightarrow$ Procuratio
  \end{itemize}
\item Confidence calibration: each rule assigned HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW based on discriminative power
\item Boilerplate filtering: rules that fire on formulaic phrases common across genres are excluded or down-weighted (e.g., ``κληρονόμοις και διαδόχοις'' appears in all property acts, not just Venditio)
\item Output: per-act evidence summary with predicted genre, confidence, and supporting phrases
\item Validated on full 907-act corpus: 540 predictions (59.5\%), 149 at HIGH confidence, no systematic false positives across 5 notaries
\end{itemize}

% ------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{NeSy Pipeline}
% ------------------------------------------------------------

\begin{itemize}
\item Four prompting strategies evaluated:
  \begin{enumerate}
  \item Zero-shot: act text + ``classify into legal genre'' instruction
  \item Few-shot: 3 annotated examples + act text
  \item Full-context: act text + full taxonomy description (192 lines)
  \item NeSy: act text + taxonomy + symbolic engine output (evidence summary with confidence scores)
  \end{enumerate}
\item NeSy injects deterministic, domain-specific knowledge into the LLM prompt as structured evidence
\item The symbolic engine runs before the LLM call (no API cost); the LLM receives the evidence as context
\item The LLM produces a structured JSON response: \{core\_layer, extension\_layer, hybrid\_tags\}
\item Temperature set to 0.0 for deterministic outputs
\end{itemize}

% ------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{Models}
% ------------------------------------------------------------

\begin{itemize}
\item 12 LLMs evaluated via OpenRouter API:
  \begin{itemize}
  \item Proprietary: Claude 3.7 Sonnet, Claude Sonnet 4, Claude Opus 4, GPT-4o, GPT-5.2 Chat
  \item Open-weight large: Llama 3.3 70B, Llama 4 Maverick (400B MoE), DeepSeek V3, DeepSeek R1, Qwen 2.5 72B
  \item Open-weight small: Llama 3.1 8B
  \item Lightweight: Gemini 2.5 Flash
  \end{itemize}
\item Total: 12 models $\times$ 4 strategies $\times$ 40 acts = 1,920 classifications
\item All runs at temperature 0.0 (deterministic)
\end{itemize}


% ============================================================
\section{Evaluation}
% ============================================================

% ------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{Setup}
% ------------------------------------------------------------

\begin{itemize}
\item Evaluation is deterministic string matching against ground truth (no LLM in the eval loop)
\item Semantic equivalence groups: ``Venditio'' = ``Sale'' = ``Πώληση'' all map to the same core category
\item Scored per tier:
  \begin{itemize}
  \item Core layer: CORRECT / INCORRECT / ERROR (JSON parse failure or API error)
  \item Extension layer: CORRECT / PARTIAL / N/A / INCORRECT
  \item Hybrid tags: CORRECT / PARTIAL / N/A / INCORRECT (set overlap)
  \end{itemize}
\item Headline metric: core layer accuracy (correct / (correct + incorrect + error))
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Results}
% ------------------------------------------------------------

Table~\ref{tab:results} reports core layer accuracy for all 12 models across 4 prompting strategies. Table~\ref{tab:full_results} presents the complete 3-tier evaluation (core, extension, hybrid) for all model--strategy combinations.

\begin{itemize}
\item Top NeSy results: GPT-4o, GPT-5.2 Chat, Llama 4 Maverick all at 72.5\%
\item Claude 3.7 Sonnet and Claude Sonnet 4 at 70.0\% with NeSy
\item NeSy is the best or tied-best strategy for 10 out of 12 models
\item Exceptions: Opus 4 prefers full-context (70.0\% vs 67.5\%), DeepSeek R1 prefers few-shot (62.5\% vs 60.0\%)
\item Extension layer: full-context and NeSy are close; full-context sometimes wins (GPT-5.2: 62.9\% vs 59.5\%) because the full taxonomy description helps with subcategory distinctions the symbolic engine does not target
\item Hybrid tags: low accuracy across the board (25--35\%), no clear strategy winner.hybrid features require clause-level legal semantics beyond formulaic phrase matching
\item NeSy still dramatically helps small models on extensions (Llama 8B: 15.4\% full-context $\rightarrow$ 48.7\% NeSy)
\end{itemize}

% ------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{Analysis}
% ------------------------------------------------------------

\begin{itemize}
\item The equaliser effect: NeSy compresses max--min gap from 47.5pp (zero-shot) to 12.5pp.symbolic knowledge injection compensates for model size and training data coverage
\item The 8B headline: Llama 3.1 8B jumps from 17.5\% (zero-shot) to 65.0\% (NeSy), a +47.5pp absolute improvement.domain-specific symbolic rules can substitute for model scale
\item The 72.5\% ceiling: three architecturally different models (GPT-4o, GPT-5.2, Llama 4 Maverick) all plateau at 72.5\%.remaining errors are likely task-inherent (ambiguous acts, annotation boundary cases, missing rule coverage) rather than model limitations
\item Reasoning models $\neq$ better: DeepSeek R1 (chain-of-thought reasoning model) performs worse with NeSy than simpler models.the symbolic evidence already provides structured reasoning; adding CoT may cause interference
\item Cost-efficiency: Llama 3.1 8B + NeSy (65.0\%) is $\sim$50$\times$ cheaper per token than Claude Opus 4 (67.5\%).practical for digital humanities projects processing thousands of acts
\item Full-context vs NeSy: providing the full taxonomy helps, but curated symbolic evidence is more effective.NeSy outperforms full-context in 10/12 models for core layer
\item Extension layer tells a different story: full-context sometimes beats NeSy (GPT-5.2: 62.9\% vs 59.5\%; Opus 4: 57.9\% vs 48.7\%; R1: 60.0\% vs 51.4\%).the full taxonomy description contains subcategory-level distinctions that the symbolic engine does not explicitly encode
\item However, NeSy still dramatically helps small models on extensions: Llama 8B goes from 15.4\% (full-context) to 48.7\% (NeSy), indicating the symbolic evidence helps the model locate the right region of the taxonomy even for finer-grained distinctions
\item Hybrid tags remain largely unsolved: 25--35\% across all models and strategies, with no clear winner.recognising cross-cutting legal features (e.g., ``cum pacto retrovendendi'', ``agricultural credit'') requires clause-level semantic understanding beyond formulaic phrase matching
\item No partial matches observed for hybrid tags (all CORRECT or INCORRECT, no PARTIAL).models either identify the exact tag set or miss entirely
\end{itemize}


% ============================================================
\section{Error Analysis}
% ============================================================

\begin{itemize}
\item Confusions cluster around semantically close categories:
  \begin{itemize}
  \item Mutuum (loan) $\leftrightarrow$ Instrumentum obligationis (debt acknowledgement).both involve debt, differ in legal framing
  \item Venditio (sale) $\leftrightarrow$ Praevenditio (forward sale).differ only in temporal aspect
  \item Emphyteusis $\leftrightarrow$ Locatio conductio rei.long-term vs short-term land use
  \end{itemize}
\item Acts with multiple legal transactions (e.g., sale + pledge) are hardest.hybrid layer helps but core classification is forced to pick one
\item The symbolic engine has no rules for rare categories (Divisio, Beneficium).these rely entirely on LLM world knowledge
\item Remaining errors at 72.5\%: approximately [N] acts are consistently misclassified across all models.candidates for re-annotation or inherent ambiguity
\end{itemize}


% ============================================================
\section{Discussion}
% ============================================================

\begin{itemize}
\item Resource contribution: first computationally accessible corpus of Heptanesian notarial acts with structured genre annotations
\item Methodological contribution: NeSy pipeline applicable to other historical document classification tasks where domain expertise can be formalised as rules
\item The symbolic engine encodes paleographic expertise in machine-readable form.a form of knowledge transfer from humanities to NLP
\item Limitations:
  \begin{itemize}
  \item 40-act benchmark is small; larger annotated set would strengthen claims
  \item Single annotator for ground truth (inter-annotator agreement not yet measured)
  \item Rules are hand-crafted and specific to Venetian-Greek notarial tradition.would need adaptation for other legal traditions
  \item Temperature 0.0 means no variance analysis across runs (single deterministic run per model)
  \end{itemize}
\item Future work:
  \begin{itemize}
  \item Annotate the full 907-act corpus using the best NeSy pipeline as pre-annotation + human correction
  \item Expand symbolic rules to cover rare categories
  \item Extend to other Venetian-ruled territories (Crete, Cyprus) with similar notarial traditions
  \item Fine-tune a small model on the annotated data as alternative to prompt-based classification
  \end{itemize}
\end{itemize}


% ============================================================
\section{Conclusion}
% ============================================================

\begin{itemize}
\item We presented HeptaNER: a corpus, benchmark, taxonomy, and neuro-symbolic pipeline for classifying Heptanesian notarial acts
\item The NeSy pipeline achieves 72.5\% accuracy, a +14.4pp improvement over the mean zero-shot baseline
\item Symbolic knowledge injection acts as an equaliser across model sizes and architectures
\item We release the corpus, benchmark, taxonomy, and symbolic engine as open resources for historical Greek NLP
\item The approach demonstrates that formalising domain expertise as symbolic rules is a cost-effective alternative to model scaling for under-resourced historical varieties
\end{itemize}
